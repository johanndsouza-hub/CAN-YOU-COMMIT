# The Consistency Experiment

## Vision

The COMMIT channel is not about weight loss, fasting, or push-ups.

It is about answering one question:

> **Can consistency change an ordinary person's life more than motivation?**

Every video is another experiment that contributes evidence toward answering that question.

The COMMIT app is the measurement tool.

The channel is the story.

---

# Core Principles

- Document experiments, not perfection.
- Show successes and failures.
- Never fake a streak.
- Missing one day is data, not failure.
- Ask questions before giving answers.
- Let evidence shape future conclusions.

---

# Video Formula

Every video should follow the same structure.

1. **Question (Hook)**
2. **The Experiment**
3. **What Happened**
4. **What I Learned**
5. **Question to the Audience**

Example:

**Hook**
> Does consistency beat motivation?

**Experiment**
> I've been testing that for 140 days...

**Evidence**
> Here's what actually happened...

**Lesson**
> This is what I learned...

**Closing Question**
> What do you think? Does consistency beat motivation?

# Long-Term Goal

Build a library of experiments that answer one question:

> **What actually happens when an ordinary person commits to showing up every day for years?**

The audience isn't following a creator.

They're participating in an experiment.

---

# Positioning Note

This shifts the channel identity from:

> The guy with streaks

To:

> The guy who tests whether streaks and consistency actually work.

That is a more durable brand because it is driven by curiosity rather than a single niche.
